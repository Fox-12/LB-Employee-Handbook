Chapter 10: Organized Play & Alliances

Contents

    Chains and Wars

    Faction activity is a real part of Torn progression, not a side issue. Members are expected to understand when chains and wars matter and to take part when required.

    Participation Standards

    Reliable participation is more valuable than occasional enthusiasm. Factions need members who can show up, follow direction, and contribute consistently.

    Alliance Rules

    Alliances matter because relationships between factions affect support, coordination, and general stability. Members should know the basic rules and avoid actions that create unnecessary problems.

    Supporting Allies

    Faction life is not only about your own direct benefit. Support goes both ways, and keeping good relationships helps the whole group operate better over time.

    Attacking Guidelines

    Members should be aware of faction rules around attacking, targets, and conduct. Acting without checking those rules first is one of the easiest ways to create avoidable issues.

    Why This Matters Long Term

    Players who understand organized play fit into faction life more easily and usually progress more smoothly because they are better connected to the systems around them.
