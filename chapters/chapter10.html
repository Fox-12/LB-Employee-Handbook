Chapter 10: Organized Play & Alliances

Contents

    Chains and Wars

    Faction activity is not optional background noise. Chains and wars are part of LB membership, and participation matters because the faction cannot operate well if members appear only when it is convenient.

    That does not mean every member contributes in the same way. It means every member is expected to respond, follow instructions, and do the part they are capable of doing.

    Alliances and Conduct

    LB is part of a wider network of relationships. Those relationships matter, and members are expected to respect them. A clear example is the rule against attacking members of the LB family.

    Faction diplomacy is not just leadership business. Individual actions can create problems for everyone else, so members need to understand that their choices carry faction consequences.

    Being Reliable in a Group

    Organized play runs better when people communicate clearly, show up prepared, and avoid creating preventable problems. This includes reading instructions before an event, keeping leadership informed if you will be absent, and not treating faction plans as suggestions.

    Reliability is one of the fastest ways to become valuable in a faction, even before your account is fully developed.
