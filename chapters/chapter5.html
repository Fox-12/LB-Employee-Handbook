Chapter 5: Common Early Mistakes

Contents

    Doing Things Out of Order

    A lot of early frustration comes from trying to solve the wrong problem first. Newer players often chase money before they have stable access to the better methods, or they spend heavily before their account is producing enough to replace what they lose.

    The result is usually the same: progress feels slow because the account is still missing the basics.

    Ignoring Tools and Instructions

    Another common mistake is treating guidance as optional. Skipping TornStats, YATA, Discord, forum guides, or faction announcements usually means asking preventable questions later and missing things that were already answered.

    LB is set up to help members move faster, but that only works if the member actually uses what is provided.

    Mistaking Activity for Progress

    Being busy is not the same as moving forward. Spending time in chat, browsing the market without a plan, or hopping between random goals can feel productive without building anything useful.

    Progress is easier to measure when your routine has a purpose: train, complete crimes, meet requirements, and improve the pieces of your account that matter next.
