Contents

    Why Companies Matter

    Player companies usually matter more than city jobs because the perks are stronger and more flexible. That does not mean every company is good for every account. What matters is whether the company matches your current stage and your actual goals.

    A company with strong late-game perks is not useful if your work stats are nowhere near what that company needs.

    Choosing a Company at the Right Stage

    Early company choices should be realistic. Low work stats limit what you can get into, and high-star positions are competitive. In many cases, the perk is your pay, not the daily wage.

    That changes how you should evaluate a job. Look at what the company will do for your training, travel, education, crime work, or income. Do not look at the wage alone.

    How to Approach Applications

    Directors want active employees who understand their own work stats and know why they are applying. Message first, include your relevant stats, and ask about training policy if needed.

    Jobs are not permanent choices. As your account changes, your best company option changes with it. Treat jobs as tools, not identity.
