Chapter 7: Crimes & Income

Contents

    Crime Progression Basics

    Crimes are one of the main progression systems tied to nerve. They help build experience, support account development, and contribute to certain faction expectations.

    Meeting Crime Requirements Efficiently

    If your faction has crime minimums, the easiest way to meet them is through regular activity rather than a rush at the end of the month. Consistency keeps the requirement light.

    Early Money Methods

    Before travel opens up, smaller city-based methods and simple reselling can help, but they are limited. These methods are useful for getting started, not for carrying the account long term.

    Building Reliable Income

    Reliable income comes from repeatable systems. The best ones are usually the ones you can keep doing without forcing your account into a bad tradeoff elsewhere.

    Balancing Crimes with Other Priorities

    Crimes matter, but they are not the only thing that matters. Income, training, travel, and faction activity all compete for attention, so your crime plan should support your broader growth rather than replace it.

    Thinking Past the Early Game

    As your account matures, crime choices become more about efficiency and fit. At that stage, what is best depends more on your goals, your faction, and the systems you are already leaning into.
