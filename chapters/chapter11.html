    Building a Stable Routine

    Long-term progress comes from routines that you can keep. Training, crimes, travel, company management, and faction participation all work better when they are built into normal play instead of treated as occasional bursts.

    A good routine is not complicated. It is repeatable, clear, and based on what your account needs next.

    Changing Priorities as You Grow

    The right focus for a new account is not the right focus forever. Early on, you are trying to unlock options and stop struggling for money. Later, you can care more about company perks, specialized training paths, and better ways to protect and use larger amounts of cash.

    Growth changes what matters. Good players adjust instead of staying attached to an old routine that no longer fits.

    What Long-Term Discipline Looks Like

    Long-term discipline is not about playing perfectly. It is about keeping your account pointed in the right direction, using your tools, and correcting mistakes before they become habits.

    That is what this handbook is meant to support. Read it, use it, and come back to the chapter you need when your account reaches the next stage.
